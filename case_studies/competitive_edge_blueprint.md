# Competitive Edge Blueprint

## Project context

This project focused on automating the creation of competitive intelligence reports. The workflow analyzed company websites, competitor context, market positioning, opportunity gaps, prioritized actions, TAM related assumptions, and strategic recommendations.

## Problem

Competitive research is valuable but often slow, inconsistent, and difficult to standardize. Analysts must collect website information, compare competitors, organize market observations, identify gaps, and translate findings into recommendations. When this work is repeated manually, report quality can vary depending on time, available context, and formatting discipline.

## Approach

The automation gathered company inputs, fetched website content, extracted structured HTML data, used AI models to generate strategic analysis, organized findings into report ready fields, applied quality control logic, and sent the final payload to Make.com for document generation.

The workflow used n8n, OpenAI models, Claude related tools, HTTP requests, HTML extraction, Google Sheets, Slack, JSON, code nodes, and Make.com document handoff.

## Solution

The final system created a repeatable competitive strategy workflow. It could collect company context, structure research inputs, generate executive style analysis, and prepare recommendations in a format suitable for a business report. The system was designed to move beyond generic summaries and produce organized sections such as opportunity gaps, prioritized actions, market opportunities, and strategic recommendations.

## My contribution

I supported the report automation architecture, data mapping, AI output structuring, quality control logic, payload preparation, troubleshooting, and the integration between analysis nodes and final document generation.

## Impact

The automation reduced the manual effort involved in preparing competitive intelligence reports and improved consistency across outputs. It also demonstrated how AI workflows can support strategy work when combined with structured inputs, clear output schemas, and review logic.
